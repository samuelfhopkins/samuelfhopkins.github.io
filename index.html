Since August 2021, I am an Assistant Professor in the mathematics department of Howard University.

I study algebraic and enumerative combinatorics. If you are also a combinatorialist, you might be interested in:

  • the Open Problems in Algebraic Combinatorics conference, which will take place in May of 2022 at the University of Minnesota;
  • the Open Problems in Algebraic Combinatorics blog.

Short bio: From September 2018 to May 2021 I was an NSF postdoc at the University of Minnesota school of mathematics, where my mentor was Vic Reiner. In June 2018 I got my PhD from the MIT math department, where my advisor was Alex Postnikov. I did my undergraduate studies at Reed College.
