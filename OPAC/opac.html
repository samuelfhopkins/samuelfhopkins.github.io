Registration: There is no registration fee for the conference, and all are invited to attend the talks. However, all participants must register by May 1, 2022 by filling out this Google form.

Arrival-Departure Dates: The expected arrival date is Sunday, May 15. The conference begins Monday morning. Most attendees will depart either Friday, May 20 or Saturday, May 21. The conference will end at 5pm on Friday.

Hotels: Hotels near campus include: The Graduate Hotel, Days Inn, and Hilton Garden Inn. Another option is Airbnb.

The above link for the Graduate Hotel includes the OPAC-specific days and discounted rate. For the Days Inn, participants can call (612) 623-9303 at any time and ask to make a reservation under "OPAC Workshop" for a discounted rate.

Local Travel Information: Most attendees will fly into the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport (MSP). The MSP airport has stops (at both terminals 1 and 2) for the light rail transit (LRT) serving Minneapolis and St. Paul. It is easy to take the light rail from MSP to campus. Taxis and Uber can also be used to get from the airport to campus.

Funding: There is limited funding available to support participants attending the conference. The deadline to apply for funding was January 15, 2022 and has passed. Funding decisions will be made soon.

Talk Titles:

  • Sami Assaf: Using polynomials to resolve open problems in representation theory and geometry
  • Sylvie Corteel: Cylindric partitions
  • Philippe Di Francesco: The combinatorics of triangular ice
  • Eugene Gorsky: Generalized q,t-Catalan polynomials and link invariants
  • June Huh: Lorentzian polynomials
  • Joel Kamnitzer: Canonical bases in representation theory and cluster algebras
  • Rick Kenyon: Dimers and related models
  • Thomas Lam: Positive geometries
  • Anton Mellit: Combinatorial expressions for the nabla operator
  • Igor Pak: Complexity aspects and challenges in algebraic combinatorics
  • Greta Panova: Structure constants in algebraic combinatorics: from Geometric Complexity Theory to Asymptotics
  • Claudiu Raicu: Homological invariants of $S_n$-equivariant monomial ideals
  • Ralf Schiffler: Perfect matching problems in cluster algebras and number theory
  • Anne Schilling: How crystals might help to make progress on plethysm coefficients

Proceedings: The conference will be accompanied by a proceedings to be published by the AMS. The proceedings articles will also be made available online here. Some of the articles are already available:

  • A positivity conjecture on the structure constants of shifted Jack functions by P. Alexandersson and V. Féray
  • The bosonic-fermionic diagonal coinvariant modules conjecture by F. Bergeron
  • $(\mathrm{GL}_k \times \mathbb{S}_n)$-modules of multivariate diagonal harmonics by F. Bergeron
  • Order polynomial product formulas and poset dynamics by S. Hopkins
  • Problems in the enumeration of tilings by T. Lai
  • Increasing subsequences and Kroenecker coefficients by J. Novak and B. Rhoades
